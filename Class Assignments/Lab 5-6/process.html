The Communication Process

Communication is a process of transmitting a message from a sender to a receiver in an understandable manner Getting the exact message to the receiver is difficult because of the environment in which communication occurs The communication environment consists of all things perceived by the senses as well as one's experiences, intelligence, and emotions.

Sender

The communication process begins with the sender who is responsible for the success of the message. The senders experiences, attitudes, knowledge. skills, perceptions. and culture influence the message. The written words, spoken words, and nonverbal language are important in ensuring the receiver interprets the message as intended by the sender.

Message

The message is the ideas the sender wants to convey to the receiver Written, oral, or nonverbal symbols are used to convey the message Channel or Medium In choosing the most appropriate channel, weigh the advantages and disadvantages of each for a particular situation. In some instances, a combination of both written admiral communication mediums will achieve the desired results.

Receiver

The receiver is the person to whom the message is sent Upon receiving the message the receiver interprets the information Feedback The feedback is the response of the receiver to the message Without feedback, the sender cannot be assured that the receiver has interpreted the message correctly.
